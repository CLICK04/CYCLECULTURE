India’s best hill stations for cycling adventures

April 03, 2025

As summer temperatures soar across India's plains, the country's hill stations offer the perfect cycling retreat. With crisp mountain air, breathtaking landscapes, and well-maintained roads, these destinations provide an unparalleled experience for cycling enthusiasts. This guide highlights the best hill stations in India where cycling infrastructure, including rental services, is well-established. Whether you're looking for a relaxing ride through tea estates or a challenging mountain climb, these locations ensure a safe and rewarding experience for all skill levels.

Munnar, Kerala (Elevation: 1,600m)

Munnar is a cyclist's paradise, with winding roads cutting through lush tea plantations. The region's pleasant summer temperatures, which rarely exceed 25°C, make it ideal for long-distance rides. One of the most scenic routes is the 22-kilometre ride from Munnar to Top Station, featuring breathtaking views and challenging gradients reaching up to 12%. Several rental shops in Munnar town offer cycles at ₹300-500 per day, with Kerala Tourism-certified outlets near Eravikulam National Park ensuring high-quality rentals. However, riders should be cautious as afternoon mists can significantly reduce visibility after 3 PM.

Coorg, Karnataka (Elevation: 1,200m)

Famous for its sprawling coffee estates and spice plantations, Coorg offers a fantastic cycling experience amidst nature. The 15-kilometre loop from Madikeri to Abbey Falls takes riders through stunning plantations and rolling terrain, making it suitable for both beginners and intermediate cyclists. With over eight rental shops in Madikeri town offering cycles for ₹250-400 per day, access to cycles is easy. Additionally, many homestays provide complimentary cycles for their guests, enhancing the cycling-friendly atmosphere of the region.

Ooty, Tamil Nadu (Elevation: 2,240m)

Ooty, nestled in the Nilgiri Hills, provides year-round cycling opportunities thanks to its cool climate and well-paved roads. A popular route is the 12-kilometre ride from Ooty to Pykara, which passes through dense eucalyptus forests and waterfalls. Government-approved rental outlets near Ooty Lake offer cycles at ₹200-350 per day, while premium mountain cycles are available at Sterling Resort. Early morning rides are recommended for a fog-free experience, ensuring clear views of the picturesque surroundings.

Shillong, Meghalaya (Elevation: 1,525m)

Often referred to as the "Scotland of the East," Shillong boasts rolling hills, rich Khasi cultural experiences, and challenging cycling routes. The 18-kilometre climb to Shillong Peak rewards riders with panoramic views of the city. Police Bazaar is home to five quality rental shops, where cycles are available for ₹400-600 per day. Meghalaya Adventures also offers guided cycling tours for those looking for a more structured experience. Cyclists are encouraged to combine their ride with a visit to the region's famous living root bridges for a truly unique adventure.

Kodaikanal, Tamil Nadu (Elevation: 2,133m)

Kodaikanal is a dream destination for cyclists, offering routes that range from short five-kilometre loops around Kodai Lake to challenging 21-kilometre ghat road climbs. The pine forest trails provide scenic beauty, while the crisp mountain air enhances the experience. Over ten rental shops near Kodai Lake provide cycles at ₹300-500 per day, with high-quality mountain cycles available near Kodaikanal International School. Riders should be aware that sharp bends along the mountain roads require skilled handling, especially on downhill stretches.

Mahabaleshwar, Maharashtra (Elevation: 1,353m)

Known for its strawberry farms, ancient temples, and stunning viewpoints, Mahabaleshwar offers a cycling experience rich in natural and cultural beauty. The region's pleasant temperatures, ranging from 18-28°C during summer, make it a great destination for cycling enthusiasts. The Table Land plateau is a popular cycling spot, offering panoramic views of the surrounding valleys. Rental options are available near Mapro Garden, with six shops offering cycles at ₹200-400 per day. Mountain cycles can also be rented from Holiday Resort for more challenging rides.

Gangtok, Sikkim (Elevation: 1,650m)

For those seeking high-altitude cycling, Gangtok presents an exhilarating experience with its winding mountain roads and breathtaking Himalayan views. Riders can explore Buddhist monasteries and serene valleys while enjoying well-maintained cycling routes. MG Marg is home to four premium rental outlets offering high-quality cycles for ₹500-800 per day, with Himalayan Cycling providing altitude-adjusted cycles for a smoother ride. Cyclists are advised to spend at least two days acclimatising before attempting long-distance or high-altitude routes.

Wayanad, Kerala (Elevation: 700-2,100m)

With its lush forests, spice plantations, and wildlife reserves, Wayanad is a haven for cyclists seeking a peaceful and immersive ride through nature. The 15-kilometre Lakkidi Loop is a particularly scenic route, passing through waterfalls and tranquil landscapes with minimal traffic. Cycle rentals are available in Kalpetta town, where seven shops offer cycles at ₹350-550 per day. For those interested in guided cycling experiences, eco-tourism centres in the region provide well-organised cycling tours.

Yercaud, Tamil Nadu (Elevation: 1,515m)

Less crowded than Ooty, Yercaud offers a relaxed cycling experience through misty hills and orange groves. Its gentle slopes make it ideal for beginners, while more challenging routes are available for experienced cyclists. The four-kilometre Lady's Seat Loop is a favourite among riders due to its wide roads and minimal traffic. Cycle rentals are available near Yercaud Lake, with three rental shops offering cycles for ₹250-400 per day. For a more premium cycling experience, GRT Nature Trails resort provides high-end cycles for guests.

Coonoor, Tamil Nadu (Elevation: 1,850m)

A quieter alternative to Ooty, Coonoor is known for its tea estate trails and serene landscapes. The seven-kilometre ride to Dolphin's Nose viewpoint offers some of the most breathtaking valley views in the region. Rental shops near Coonoor Station provide cycles at ₹300-450 per day, and some tea estates include cycle rentals as part of their guided tours. With its peaceful environment and less crowded routes, Coonoor is a must-visit for cyclists looking to escape the hustle and bustle of larger hill stations.

Essential Cycling Tips for Hill Stations

Cyclists visiting high-altitude destinations should allow at least one to two days for acclimatisation before attempting long rides. It's essential to check the condition of rental cycles, particularly brakes and gear mechanisms, to ensure a safe ride. Helmets are mandatory at most rental shops and should always be worn for safety. Since mountain weather can be unpredictable, carrying lightweight rain gear is recommended. To make the most of the experience, cyclists should start their rides by 7 AM, avoiding peak afternoon mist and potential visibility issues. Additionally, monsoon months should be avoided due to slippery roads and increased risk of landslides.

Conclusion

India's hill stations provide some of the most exciting and scenic cycling experiences, offering everything from gentle plantation loops to challenging mountain ascents. With established rental networks, well-maintained routes, and cool weather, these destinations present a refreshing alternative to cycling in the plains. Whether you're looking for a casual ride or an endurance challenge, these locations cater to all levels of cyclists. Ready to explore?
